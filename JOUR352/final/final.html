The Mental Health Crisis of America’s Healthcare Workers

In most healthcare systems and across all fields of medicine, doctors have been found to have higher rates of depression and anxiety in comparison to the general population and other professional groups.

The work done by physicians, nurses, and other healthcare workers is often glamorized because of the financial stability and rewarding pay which it produces. What many fail to consider is the mental stress which these positions produce and the emotional baggage which healthcare professionals are forced to carry.

Working in the medical field has historically been considered a high-stress profession. For decades, doctors have dealt with higher depression and burnout. This mental health crisis healthcare workers are facing was amplified during the pandemic:

The Burnout of our Nations Healthcare Workers


  • The term burnout describes a state in which one experiences complete mental, physical, and emotional exhaustion.
  • Burnout is commonly produced when one constantly feels swamped and overwhelmed with stress.
  • Burnout is often expeireinced by healthcare providers due to the intense, pressure filled environment they are required to work under.
  • In our nation, healthcare workers are burning out at alarming rates.
  • Healthcare workers who experienced harassment were more likely to report burnout, depression, and anxiety, compared with those who did not.
  • A study released by the CDC found that in 2022, 46% of health workers reported feeling burned out often or very often compared to 32% in 2018.
